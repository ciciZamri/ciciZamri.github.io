E-commerce Business Strategies, Summary of chapter 5

Last updated: 3 November 2020 by Muhammad Hammizie

Business Model

Business model is a company's core strategy for profitably doing business. It describes the rationale of how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value, in economic, social, cultural or other contexts.

E-commerce business model

E-commerce company leverages the Internet and Web technology in their strategy for profitably doing business.

8 key elements of business model

Value proposition

A promise of value that a company will deliver and how it will fulfills the needs of customers.

Revenue model

Company's plan on how to generate financial income.

Market opportunity

The revenue potential within a company's intended marketspace

Competitive environment

Direct and indirect competitors doing business in the same marketspace.

Competitive advantage

The factors that differentiate the business from its competitors, enabling it to provide better products with lower cost.

Market strategy

The plan a company develops that outlines how it will enter a market and attract customers.

Organizational development

The process of defining all the functions within a business and the skills necessary to perform each job.

Management team

A group of individuals at the highest level of management of a comapny who have the day-to-day tasks of guiding the company's growth and expansion.

B2C business model

E-tailer

The online version of traditional retailer. Use Internet as a medium to provide products and services to customers.

Community provider

Provides an online community of like-minded individuals for networking and information sharing. Revenue is generated by advertising, referral fees and subscriptions.

Content provider

Information and entertainment companies that provide digital content such as news, musics and videos.

Portal

Offers powerful search tools that provides a portal for consumers to gain access to content or services.

Transaction broker

Processes online sales transaction and utilize transaction fees as a source of revenue.

Market creator

Use Internet to create markets that bring buyers and sellers together.

Service provider

Offers online services.

B2B business model

E-distributor

Supplies products directly to individual businesses

E-procurement

Single firms create digital markets for thousands of sellers and buyers.

Exchange

Independantly owned digital marketplace for direct inputs, usually for a vertical industry group.

Industry consortium

Industry-owned vertical digital market.

Private industrial network

Industry-owned private industrial network that coordinates supply chains with a limited set of partners.
