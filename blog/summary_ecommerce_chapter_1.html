E-commerce Summary of chapter 1

Last updated: 20 October 2020 by Muhammad Hammizie

E-commerce

E-commerce or electronic commerce is the activity of selling and buying goods or services over the Internet. E-commerce involves digitally enabled commercial transactions between and among organizations and individuals.

E-business

E-business refers primaryly to the digital enabling of transactions and processes within a firm, involving infomation systems under the control of firm.

Technological building blocks of E-commerce

Internet

Internet is the a global computer network that let billions of electronic devices around the world to communicate with each other. Internet is the main technology that enable e-commerce activity.

World Wide Web

The World Wide Web or Web is an information system where documents and other web resources are identified and interlinked by Uniform Resource Locators(URL) and can be accessed via the Internet. Most modern e-commerce organizations use World Wide Web for its online transactions.

Mobile Apps

Mobile application is a computer program or software application designed to run a mobile devices. Mobile apps has changed the e-commerce industry as the process of e-commerce activity has become much easier and faster with mobile apps.

E-commerce unique features

Ubiquity

available just about everywhere, at all times, making it possible for all platforms

Global reach

permits commercial transactions to cross cultural and national boundaries far more conveniently and cost-dffectively

Universal standards

shared by all nations around the world, in contrast to most traditional commerce technologies, which differ from one nation to the next

Richness

enables an online merchant to deliver marketing messages in a way not possible wit traditional commerce technologies

Interactivity

allows for two-way communication between merchant and consumer and enables the merchant to engage a consumer in ways similar to a face-to-face experience, but on much more massive, global scale

Information density

The total amount of quality of information available to all market participants.

Personalization and customization

The increase in information density allows merchants to target their marketing messages to specific individuals.

Social technology

Provides a many-to-many model of mass communications. Millions of of users are able to generate content consumed by millions of other users. The result is the formation of social networks on a wide scale and the aggregation of large audiences on social network platforms.

Types of E-commerce

Business to Consumer(B2C)

involves business selling to consumers and is the type of e-commerce that most consumers are likely to encounter

Business to Business(B2B)

involves businessess selling to the other businesses and is the largest form of e-commerce.

Consumer to Consumer(C2C)

Consumer sell and buy with each other

Social E-commerce

E-commerce that is enabled by social network technologies

M-commerce

Th use of wireless digital devices to enable online transactions

Local E-commerce

Focused on engaging the consumer based on his or her current geographic location
